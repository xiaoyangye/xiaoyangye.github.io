Hi there, my name is Xiaoyang Ye (叶晓阳), pronounced "Hsiao-young Yay!"

I am an economist at Amazon's Stores Economics and Science (SEAS) central science team, building the foundational economic models for amazon.com using causal ML and behavioral economics.

I previously worked at PXT Central Science and led the science behind Amazon's major people engagement programs.

My academic research (not affiliated with Amazon) focuses on optimizing human decisions from school to the workforce.

I was a postdoc with Susanna Loeb at Brown and with Adam Kapor, Chris Neilson, and Seth Zimmerman at Princeton.

I received my Ph.D. under the advising of Steve DesJardins, Sue Dynarski, and Brian Jacob from the University of Michigan.

Why do I want to improve human capital accumulation for the poor? One reason: "25 dangerous roads to school in the world."
