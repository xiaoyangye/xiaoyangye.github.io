I am a postdoc with Adam Kapor, Christopher Neilson, and Seth Zimmerman in the Woodrow Wilson School at Princeton in 2019-20. I will be a postdoc with Susanna Loeb in the Annenberg Institute at Brown from 2020.

I work on the economics of education, with a particular interest in understanding and improving public policy to increase readiness, access, choice, match, and success in higher education to reduce poverty and inequality.

I received my Ph.D. in Higher Education (economics and K-20 policy concentrations) from the University of Michigan in 2019.

Why do I want to improve education policy and school effectiveness for the poor? One reason: "25 dangerous roads to school in the world."

Research Projects
